[The ENIAC]
So the US Department Of Defence decided to make a machine that can perform mathematical calculations in seconds
they called it the "ENIAC"
(Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer)

ENIAC's design and construction was financed by the United States Army
The total cost was about $487,000, equivalent to $6,600,000 in 2022. The construction contract was signed on June 5, 1943
work on the computer began in secret at the University of Pennsylvania's Moore School of Electrical Engineering the following month,
under the code name "Project PX"

ENIAC was designed by Ursinus College physics professor John Mauchly
and J. Presper Eckert of the University of Pennsylvania, U.S.


ENIAC contained 18,000 vacuum tubes, 70,000 resistors, 
10,000 capacitors, and approximately 5,000,000 hand-soldered joints. 
It weighed more than 30 short tons (27 tons),
was roughly 8 ft (2 m) tall, 3 ft (1 m) deep, and 100 ft (30 m) long,
occupied 300 sq ft (28 m2) and consumed 150 kW of electricity

it could perform Multiplication of two 10-digit numbers in 2.8 Seconds
and Add 5000 numbers in 1 Second
ENIAC was an engineering masterpiece




[Transistors]
ENIAC was discontinued in 1956, as a new technology was being developed

The Bipolar Transistor invented in 1947
Compared to vacuum tubes
they are much smaller
require less power than vacuum tubes
and give off less heat

and gave rise to The "Second Generation" of Computers




[The Traitorous Eight]
Meanwhile....
in 1957
at Shockley Semiconductor Laboratory, William Shockley recruited a group of young PhD graduates,
with the goal to develop new semiconductor devices.

Shockley was an experienced researcher and teacher,
and had even recieved a Nobel Prize in Physics,
what he wasnt, however, was being good at management, he was really bad at it
so bad that one physicist swore that :

"Shockley could see electrons ,but had trouble seeing people"
and,
"A disasterously bad businessman, and a worse manager"

all this lead to 8 of the PhD students to leave Shockley Semiconductor Laboratory,
and start their own company ,Fairchild Semiconductor

Shockley described their leaving as "Betrayal"
leading to the group being known as The Traitorous Eight

one of those eight students was Robert Noyce,
who in 1959 made the first truely monolithic IC chip,
which revolutionized computer hardware,
leading to the "Third Generation" of computer technology
